10.632-8.834z21:Teb9,<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Wreno’s <strong>Vendor Service Area</strong> system allows Property Managers to discover Vendors based on geographic coverage — filtering by state, county, city, or even custom regions. Vendors define where they operate, and our system intelligently matches them to projects and compliance workflows.</p>
<p>This feature dramatically improved Vendor discoverability and opened up new layers of automation across our platform — from onboarding to compliance targeting.</p>
<hr>
<h2>System Design &#x26; Architecture</h2>
<p>We needed a geospatial solution that was both accurate and scalable. After researching multiple options — including building a full U.S. location hierarchy from U.S. Census TIGER/Line shapefiles — we ultimately integrated <strong>Boundaries.io</strong>, which provided the precision and performance we needed without heavy maintenance overhead.</p>
<p>On the backend, we used:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>PostGIS</strong> on top of <strong>PostgreSQL</strong> for advanced spatial queries</li>
<li><strong>GeoJSON</strong> to store and transmit Vendor service areas</li>
<li><strong>Google Places API</strong> for location autocomplete and geocoding</li>
<li><strong>Boundaries.io</strong> to resolve cities, counties, and zip codes to shapes</li>
</ul>
<p>The frontend leveraged React and dynamic filtering to let users explore by region, while backend controllers handled spatial lookups to find Vendors operating within the target area.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Key Features</h2>
<h3>📍 Spatial Matching</h3>
<p>We used PostGIS functions like <code>ST_Contains</code> and <code>ST_Intersects</code> to determine which Vendors covered a given location. This enabled:</p>
<ul>
<li>Matching Vendors to projects by market, city, or zip code</li>
<li>Quickly filtering out Vendors outside a project’s service area</li>
<li>Ensuring that Property Managers only see Vendors where they operate</li>
</ul>
<h3>🔍 Vendor Discovery</h3>
<p>Property Managers can now filter and sort Vendors not just by credentials and performance — but by where they actually do work. This opened up huge gains in accuracy and usability across our Vendor search experience.</p>
<h3>🌐 Flexible Geodata Input</h3>
<p>Vendors can define service areas in a variety of ways:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cities, states, or counties (resolved via Boundaries.io)</li>
<li>Zip codes</li>
<li>Custom shapes (GeoJSON polygons)</li>
</ul>
<p>This flexibility helped us support both small local providers and national operators with complex footprints.</p>
